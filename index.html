Japan's 2011 9.0 Earthquake

Geology:

Earthquake definition: a sudden and violent shaking of the ground,
sometimes causing great destruction,
as a result of movements within the earth's crust or volcanic action.
Plate Information: Pacific Plate, The plates are convergent

Spheres:

The 9.1 Earthquake that happend underneath japan had a major imapact on the different spheres of the earth

Geosphere: The Earthquake was caused by a convergent plate boundary. Northeast japan's infrastructure was devastated

Biosphere: Humans, plants, and animals lost their lives during the Earthquake

Hydrosphere: The Earthquake struck 15 miles under the ocean, which caused a tsunami to occur

Atmosphere: The Earthquake caused physical damage to a nuclear power plant which caused pollution and radiation.

Economical:

The Earthquake had a major negative impact on japan's economy.
The damage caused costed japan about 309 billion U.S dollars.
163 countries and regions, and 42 international organizations offered to help japan

Preparation:

Stay out of damaged buildings, Stay away from damaged areas,
Indoors: Stay beneath a sturdy piece of furniture or against a door
Outdoors: Move into the open, stay away from buildings and traffic lights.
